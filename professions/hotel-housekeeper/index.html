About This Job

A hotel housekeeper is responsible for ensuring guest rooms and common areas are clean, tidy, and well-maintained in hotels, resorts, or other lodging facilities. Common tasks include making beds, replacing linens and towels, dusting, vacuuming, restocking amenities, cleaning bathrooms, and reporting maintenance issues.

This is a dependable entry-level job with steady demand in the hospitality industry across North America. It does not require formal education or certification, making it ideal for newcomers or international job seekers. Employers look for punctuality, attention to detail, time management, and the ability to follow hygiene and safety standards. Basic English skills are helpful for reading instructions and communicating with guests or supervisors.
